Large Assortment

Independent assortment, a core principle of genetics, describes the random distribution of alleles (gene versions) into gametes (sperm and egg cells) during sexual reproduction. This means that the inheritance of one trait does not influence the inheritance of another trait, leading to a vast array of possible gene combinations in offspring. Essentially, it's the reason why siblings can look so different, even from the same parents.
